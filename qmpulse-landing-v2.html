The flow

One milestone, one continuous heartbeat.

The PMO opens it, the PMO closes it — and in between, every team hands off cleanly, gets notified at each beat, and works from the same live data. Risk is tracked start to finish.

01

Plan the milestone

PMO

Every cycle starts with the PMO. They create the milestone — scope, target dates and test environments — and the teams involved are notified the moment the clock starts.

MilestonesConfigurationPM Dashboard
02

Author requirements

FA

Functional Analysts capture the requirements for the milestone. The AI Requirement Analyzer works alongside the author and reviewing FAs, flagging vague or untestable specs before they spread.

RequirementsAI Requirement Analyzer
03

Peer-approve

FA

A second FA reviews and approves each requirement — never its own author, and only from within the same project. Segregation of duties is enforced, not assumed.

Requirement approvalSame-project reviewer
04

Assign & build

Dev LeadDev

The Dev Lead is notified to assign approved requirements across the team. Developers build, and flip each item to "For QA Test" when it's ready — while the QA Lead is notified in parallel to start planning tests.

Task assignmentStatus: For QA TestNotifications
05

Author test cases

QA

In parallel with development, QA writes test cases mapped to each requirement — by hand or AI-assisted — so the moment a build lands, the tests are already waiting.

Test CasesAI AuthoringTraceability
06

Execute testing

QA

Once a developer hands a ticket to QA, execution runs per environment. Pass, fail and blocked update live; defects are raised straight from the failing step and pushed back to Dev through Redmine.

Execution DashboardDefectsRedmine write-back
07

Close testing & track

QA

QA ends testing and updates their tasks against the milestone. Is it 100% clean, or are blockers still open? The milestone tells the truth at a glance — for everyone watching.

MilestonesTasksQA Analytics
08

UAT, close & learn

UATPMO

UAT runs to sign-off. The PMO marks UAT complete, then completes and closes the milestone — and lessons learned are captured so the next cycle starts sharper than the last.

The backbone

Redmine-synced. AI-accelerated.

Two forces run through every stage: your tracker stays the system of record, and AI removes the busywork between the beats.

Deep Redmine sync

QMPulse doesn't replace Redmine — it makes it flow. Tickets, projects and custom fields move both ways, so your tracker and your quality workspace never drift apart.

  • Import requirements and tickets, hierarchy intact
  • Push defects and status changes straight back
  • Insert-only sync — nothing gets clobbered
  • Custom-field mapping across both systems

The AI Hub

AI is woven through the lifecycle, not tacked on the side — flagging risk early, drafting the tedious parts, and answering questions in plain language.

  • Requirement Analyzer — catches vague, untestable specs
  • AI test-case generation from requirements
  • QA Copilot — ask anything about your workspace
  • Requirement Chat — converse with your specs
